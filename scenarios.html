Scenarios

To demonstrate the capabilities of GLIMPSE, we have developed four scenarios that are meant to illustrate US policy options from 2005 -- 2055.

  • Rollback to 2005: All policies put into effect after 2005 are revoked. Emissions grow as population and activities grows over time.
  • Air quality policy: Current air quality policies remain in effect. Large reductions in air pollutants; growth in greenhouse gases.
  • Greenhouse gas mitigation: CO2 emissions are reduced at the rate of 1% per year, leading to 50% reduction by 2055. This also leads to air quality improvements, but their implementation is slower.
  • Air quality and greenhouse gas policy: Both implementation of current air quality rules and 1% per year reduction in CO2.

    • For each scenario, we calculate the change in climate and human health impacts, relative to the Rollback to 2005 scenario. Each of the metrics are described in more detail below.

While the air quality scenario causes a substantial decrease in human health damages, this scenario also leads to an increase in warming. The greenhouse gas scenario has very little health benefits in 2020, because the emission reductions are phased in gradually.

TIRF50

The total integrated radiative forcing (TIRF) over the 50 years from 2005 -- 2055. This is a measure of the climate impacts of the short-lived climate forcers.

GWP100

The global warming potential over the next 100 years. This is a measure of the climate impacts of the greenhouse gases.

Health Costs 2020

The human health costs of air pollution in the year 2020 of the scenario.
